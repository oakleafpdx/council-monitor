## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This work session focused on the Public Works Service Area budget for FY 26-27, presenting stark financial challenges across all four bureaus (Water, BES, PBOT, and Parks). Deputy City Administrator Priya Dhanapal led the presentation, outlining a combined $4.7 billion annual budget managing $77 billion in infrastructure assets with $14.2 billion in deferred maintenance. The session revealed significant structural funding gaps: PBOT faces the largest shortfall at $25 million in general transportation revenue, while Water and BES each face approximately $7 million gaps, and Parks shows a $7 million current service level gap.

Council members engaged in detailed questioning about rate scenarios, maintenance backlogs, and service reduction options. **Mitch Green (District 4)** [01:01:12] explicitly rejected Water Bureau Scenario 3, stating he's "not supportive of Scenario 3" due to concerns about increasing operations and maintenance liabilities. **Jamie Dunphy (District 1)** [01:11:16] requested district-by-district breakdowns of maintenance backlogs, emphasizing equity concerns about service distribution. The discussion highlighted the fundamental challenge facing all bureaus: costs rising faster than revenues, with limited options beyond service cuts or revenue increases.

The tone was serious and analytical, with councilors grappling with difficult trade-offs between affordability and service levels. **Steve Novick (District 3)** [01:25:14] noted that nearly $8 million of PBOT's flexible spending goes to newer programs like derelict RV and campsite cleanup that didn't exist a decade ago. The presentation emphasized that decisions made in this budget cycle will impact service delivery and infrastructure condition for decades, given the long lifespan of public works assets.
