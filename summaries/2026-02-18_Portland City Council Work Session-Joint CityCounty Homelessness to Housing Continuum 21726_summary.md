## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The City of Portland and Multnomah County held a comprehensive joint work session to examine their respective roles and responsibilities in the regional homelessness response system. The meeting featured detailed presentations on the continuum of services from prevention through housing placement, with staff from both jurisdictions outlining current programs and funding flows. The discussion came at a critical juncture as both governments face severe budget constraints with the expiration of ARPA and other one-time funding sources.

Key themes emerged around the need to move from coordination to consolidation of services, eliminate duplicative programs, and establish clearer role definitions between the jurisdictions. Candace Avalos (District 1) emphasized that the city should focus on "bricks and sticks" - housing production and homeownership - while stepping back from shelter operations [08:22]. Multiple speakers, including Mitch Green (District 4), called for the city to become a "price maker, not price taker" in the rental market through social housing acquisition [01:09:25].

The session revealed significant tension over Multnomah County's proposed closure of over 1,000 shelter beds due to budget shortfalls, with several councilors expressing concern about the community impact. Jillian Shoney, Director of the Homelessness Response System, provided stark assessments of the fiscal crisis, noting that the system spends approximately $150 million annually on shelter between both jurisdictions [01:21:05]. The meeting concluded with calls for staff recommendations on consolidation and clearer policy direction from elected officials.
