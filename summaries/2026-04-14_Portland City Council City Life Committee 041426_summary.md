## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The inaugural meeting of Portland's City Life Committee convened to establish the committee's mission and hear from community stakeholders across three thematic areas: economic development, community resilience, and the experience of residents and visitors. Committee Chair Candace Avalos (District 1, Housing & Permitting Committee Chair) [00:03:55] opened by noting this committee combines work from three separate committees under the new form of government, creating "a new space for our council."

The meeting featured 15 panelists across three panels providing diverse perspectives on Portland's current challenges and opportunities. The economic development panel highlighted stark contrasts between Multnomah County's job losses (10,000 in one year) [00:12:42] and neighboring Clark County's growth, sparking debate about tax policy impacts versus other factors. The community resilience panel emphasized the interconnected nature of climate action, housing, and economic stability. The final panel on resident and visitor experiences focused heavily on childcare infrastructure, immigrant community safety, arts funding, parks programming, and worker concerns.

A notable exchange occurred between Dan Ryan (District 2) and economic panelists Dr. Mary King and Andrew Hone regarding explanations for the stark economic performance gap between Multnomah and Clark Counties [00:39:25]. The meeting concluded with an ordinance related to Comcast franchise amendments following the dissolution of the Mount Hood Cable Regulatory Commission.

The tone was collaborative and information-gathering, with committee members asking clarifying questions rather than taking strong policy positions. Chair Avalos emphasized the committee's commitment to ongoing public engagement, announcing that future meetings will begin with 15-minute public communication periods.
