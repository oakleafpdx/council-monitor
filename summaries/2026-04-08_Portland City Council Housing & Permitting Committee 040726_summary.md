EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The Portland City Council Housing and Permitting Committee held its inaugural meeting on April 7, 2026, chaired by Mayor Keith Wilson with Vice Chair Mitch Green (District 4). The committee addressed two major housing finance items: implementing Oregon's Moderate Income Revolving Loan (MURL) program and reallocating $56 million in previously unspent Portland Housing Bureau funds. The MURL ordinance passed committee 4-1 after heated debate over governance structure and unit minimums, with amendments lowering the threshold from 75 to 20 units and expanding income eligibility from 60-120% AMI to 50-120% AMI. The more contentious $56 million supplemental budget sparked extensive public testimony and multiple council amendments, reflecting urgent concerns about Portland's escalating housing crisis.

Public testimony revealed deep frustration over "hidden" housing funds sitting unallocated while homelessness increased 25% year-over-year. Speakers emphasized prevention over crisis response, with multiple advocates noting eviction prevention costs significantly less than rehousing. The committee heard passionate testimony supporting social housing acquisition, debt buydowns for affordable properties, and equitable distribution of rental assistance, particularly in underserved East Portland. Several amendments were proposed by committee members, with particular focus on targeting deeply affordable supportive housing and adding specific projects.

The meeting demonstrated the new committee's collaborative but sometimes tense dynamics, with Vice Chair Green expressing concerns about accountability and governance structure that led to his dissenting vote on MURL. Mayor Wilson emphasized urgency throughout, noting the supplemental budget must advance quickly to be included in the Technical Adjustment Ordinance. The extensive public testimony - over 30 speakers - underscored community demand for immediate action on housing resources.
