## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The April 15th Portland City Council meeting began with a pre-gavel celebration recognizing National Public Safety Telecommunications Week, honoring BOEC's 911 dispatchers and call takers who achieved international accreditation. The meeting then proceeded with the controversial Technical Adjustment Ordinance (TAO) for FY 2025-26, which dominated proceedings with heated debates over housing funding allocations. Multiple amendments were proposed and failed, including attempts by Dan Ryan [00:25:25] to prioritize rent assistance for those with eviction notices, and several iterations by Loretta Smith [00:26:32] to restore $2.5 million in funding for Self Enhancement Inc. and Williams & Russell homeownership programs that had been removed in committee.

The TAO debate exposed significant tensions over racial equity and housing policy, with Loretta Smith (District 1) [01:31:33] expressing fury over the removal of funding for Black organizations while keeping other projects. Candace Avalos (District 1, Housing & Permitting Committee Chair) [01:13:37] opposed amendments that would add barriers to rental assistance programs. The underlying TAO ultimately passed 8-3 despite the failed amendments. The council also approved a resolution directing staff to accelerate Inner East Side area planning for increased housing density, with a 90-day timeline for preliminary zoning concepts.

The meeting revealed deep divisions on housing policy, racial equity considerations, and governance processes, with multiple councilors criticizing the lack of consistent vetting procedures for project-specific allocations. The lengthy debates over relatively small funding amounts highlighted broader systemic issues in Portland's housing policy and budget processes.

## TOPIC FLAGS

### Real Estate & Housing
**Discussion:** Extensive debate over housing funding allocations, including $8.8 million for rent buydowns, rental assistance programs, and homeownership development funding. The removal of funding for Self Enhancement Inc. and Williams & Russell sparked heated exchanges about racial equity in housing policy.

**Key Participants:** Loretta Smith [01:31:33] argued passionately for Black homeownership programs, noting 26% homeownership rate for Black residents vs. 53% for white residents. Candace Avalos (District 1, Housing & Permitting Committee Chair) [01:13:37] opposed adding barriers to rental assistance. Eric Zimmerman [01:49:12] supported reducing rent buydown funding.

**Decisions:** TAO passed without additional funding for specific homeownership projects, maintaining focus on rent assistance and buydown programs.

**Implications:** Reflects ongoing challenges in Portland's housing policy coordination and racial equity considerations in development funding.

### Land Use & Zoning
**Discussion:** Council approved resolution to accelerate Inner East Side area planning with 90-day timeline for preliminary zoning concepts to increase housing density.

**Key Participants:** Angelita Morillo (District 3) [02:31:04] explained the resolution requires factual findings on infrastructure capacity and displacement risk before zoning changes. Mitch Green (District 4) [02:33:36] emphasized urgency to avoid being a barrier to housing production.

**Decisions:** Resolution approved directing staff to develop zoning change proposals by June 30, 2027.

**Implications:** Could significantly increase housing capacity in transit-rich areas, representing major opportunity for infill development.
