So the state legislature passed a bill in 2024 that established a Moderate Income Revolving Loan Fund, or MERL, to help cities finance housing development for households earning between 60% and 120% of area median income. This ordinance establishes Portland's local implementation of the state's moderate income revolving loan program, a new financing tool created by the legislature to support housing for middle-income households. This proposal allows the city to access no-interest state funds and deploy them locally using Prosper Portland to provide gap financing to housing projects that would not otherwise be financially feasible. Importantly, this tool leverages future tax revenue to support housing production today without requiring direct general fund investment. It also aligns state resources with our local tools like tax increment financing to accelerate the development of much-needed housing near transit and in high-opportunity areas. Adopting this ordinance is necessary— is a necessary step for Portland to access these limited first-come, first-served state resources and ensure that we can provide— that we can bring forward projects that expand housing options for our workforce and middle-income residents.
